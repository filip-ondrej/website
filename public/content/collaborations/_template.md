## How It Started

This is the opening beat of the story — one or two short paragraphs. How did the collaboration begin, what was the context, what was at stake? Keep paragraphs to 2–3 sentences; the modal typography is built for rhythm, not walls of text.

A second paragraph can add the human detail that makes it memorable.

## The Work

Describe the actual work. Use **bold** for the load-bearing phrases and keep claims backed by numbers where possible.

- Bullet lists render as styled story lists
- Use them for scope: what was designed, built, programmed
- Three to five bullets is the sweet spot

### A Sub-Chapter If Needed

H3 headings work for sub-structure inside a chapter.

## The Turning Point

Every good collaboration story has one moment where it could have gone wrong — tell it here. Third-party validation beats self-praise:

> "A quote from someone else — a supervisor, a judge, a newspaper — carries more weight than any claim you make about yourself."

![A video embed works the same way — just use a Vimeo or YouTube link](https://vimeo.com/1132746110)

Close with the outcome and the arc: what changed because of this collaboration, what it taught, and where it points next.
